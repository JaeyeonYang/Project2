Introduction:  
The Zhong Shao Lab is a global leader in formal methods, programming languages, and certified system software. The lab’s research is centered on developing mathematically rigorous approaches to software correctness, with a particular focus on building operating systems, OS kernels, and hypervisors that are provably secure and reliable. Professor Shao’s pioneering work on type-preserving compilation for Standard ML and the FLINT certifying infrastructure has set the foundation for modern approaches to program correctness and bug-free software.

A hallmark of the lab’s approach is the use of formal verification and type theory to ensure that every layer of system software—from abstraction layers to system libraries—meets strict correctness guarantees. Current research extends these techniques to the design and implementation of certified OS kernels and hypervisors, aiming to eliminate entire classes of software vulnerabilities. The lab’s vision is to transform the way system software is built, making mathematically proven security and reliability a standard for the next generation of computing infrastructure. Through collaboration, education, and innovation, the Shao Lab is shaping the future of trustworthy and secure computer systems.
